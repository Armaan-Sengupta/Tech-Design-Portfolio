The Design Process

This section highlights an instance when I have followed the design process very well. In this case, it was for our first major project, the hot glue gun holder. Considering the fact that the primary purpose of the assignment was to learn the design process, it is fitting that I followed it well. Scroll down to find out more.

The Process

This is a short version (2/5) of the process I followed in the creation of my product

Understand the situation

The first step in the process required understanding the task that had to be accomplished. This included understanding external and personal design constraints and goals. After these goals are set, with the constraints in mind, I researched what had already been done to solve the problem. I took inspiration as well as took note of the faults that my design could improve upon.

Brainstorm

After understanding what needed to be done and what was already done, I started to brainstorm designs that met the goals while staying within the constraints. The most promising ideas where sketched.

Construct a Prototype

After choosing the best design from the list of sketches, it was time to construct a working prototype. The model was made without the modification of any parts, and in fact used a modular, adaptable building system so that no materials would be wasted on the prototype. This was done so that when the prototype needed to be finalized, the same materials could easily be re-used.

Testing and Evalutation

After the construction, it was time to test the product. During testing of the product, I noticed deficiencies in the product that affected its core function. I identified the cause of the defect and understood how it affected the outcome.

Do More Research

After the failure of the product, I restarted the spice cycle. This meant doing more research. I tried to look at it from a different angle. This, for me, meant not trying to research my product directly but rather what I was trying to accomplish in general. By doing so, I found clever methods that professional industries used to achieve similar effects, for different reasons, in a very successful manner.

Brainstorm

I now needed to adapt the industry technique to fit my application. I needed it to match all of my goals and stays within my constraints. So I started brainstorming modifications to the industrial design that would make it fit better with the current objective.

Construct a Prototype

To test if the new design fixed all the issues that plagued the previous one, I had to build it. Again not sure whether or not this was the final version, I did not modify materials and used the same adaptable system as the first prototype.

Evaluate

This new design had fixed all the problems that were identified with the original prototype. It was now significantly better than the original and had met all the goals stated in the beginning. Now new problems had arisen, however, they were quite minor in scale. With this, I decided to make the final version remembering of my constrains was time.

Construction

With this being the final version, the adaptable material was finally cut to the correct and optimal size. The product was also painted and more permanently fastened together. The final product was ready to present.

Evaluation/Presentation

With the final product made, it was now left for the consumers and industry to judge. In this case, since I had kept strict regulation to my goals and constraints, it was widely liked by classmates, both for its functionality and novelty. All in all, it was a success.

I followed the design process particularly well in this project. In fact, as to not make this page too long, most of the iteration is cut out.
