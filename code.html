Coding

I have been coding for a long time. I first started dabbling with programming towards the beginning of Grade 6. Since then, I have learned a lot, improving my skills any way I can. I have done software application programming, electronics programming, robotics programming, game development, and competitive programming. In our grade 10 tech design class, we focused on electronics programming. We programmed the Arduino with various different components. Since I had already worked with all these components, I decided to take each project to another level, often making the circuits and code more interesting. Also, for the tutorials, I tried to get more in-depth, by either coding everything from scratch or explaining every line in the code thoroughly. Below I explain what I did V.S what was asked in an effort to show how I went above and beyond.

Driveway Band

My circuit and code: In this project, I make a music maker (hence the Garage band rip off). You have a sequence of notes (13), and for each, you can change the frequency of the note as well as how long it is played for. You can also use any of the note spaces for a pause. With this technique, you can make simple and catchy beats. If you would like more space, all that needs to be done is add more slide switches; this will exponentially increase the amount of memory you have (since it adds a new digit). The video below explains this better.

Required Circut and code: A piezo buzzer that changes pitch depending on the amount of ambient light. The ambient light can be changed by bringing your hand closer or further from the photocell. 

Choose the right Color

My circuit and code: In this tutorial, I make a memory and speed game with LEDs and a push-button where you are shown a random colour in the beginning, and you have to press the button when that colour shows up again. This is a fun game to play, and you can easily change the difficulty if you find it too easy or too hard.

Required circut and code: Use 3 buttons, where each button controls either the red, green, or blue part of the RGB spectrum. By holding the buttons, you can increment the value (it loops back) and hence set any colour on an RGB LED. 

Missile Launch

My circuit and my code: In this project, I use slide switches and LED's to emulate the launch sequence for a missile, or at least a part of it. You have to press two buttons within a short duration of each other and keep them pressed down for the entire duration. Then and only then will the missile launch, or in our case, the final LED turns on.

Required circuit and code: Have two pushbuttons, one turns an LED on and the other turns it off.

Light Bounce

My circuit and code: In this tutorial, I create a propagating or bouncing effect LED. I also use a potentiometer to control the speed at which the light bounces. This was a fun project, and you can watch me as I wire and code the circuit from scratch.     

Required circuit and code: Make an LED blink
