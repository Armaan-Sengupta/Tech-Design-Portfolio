CAD - 3D Models

Generating a detailed 3D model with CAD software requires that every measurement be exact, and every detail be correct. This was the case when I designed a 1200sqft house. The house had a detailed and intricate placement of every component, whether that was a door, a window, a fridge, or a stairway. Every detail was thought of, including interactions with the sun, private v.s public areas, accessibility, location, and aesthetics. Pictures and videos of this final model as well as four other models that I made, will be shown below.

The 3D model of the house was detailed and thoughtful because it had to be. The house had to be designed with constraints such as square footage, number of floors, building codes, and what could realistically be made in a real-life model. It also had to be designed with criteria such as private and public spaces, placement of furniture for the most efficient utilization, bathrooms on different floors being close so plumbing is easy, size of hallways concerning the human body, window placement for optimum sunlight, and much, much more. Because of this, the model was made exactly correct with the correct scale and dimensions, while keeping in mind our constraints and criteria. 

Some improvements I would make in the future would be to add smaller-scale furniture such as flower pots, and nightlamps. This would make the house feel more lived in and realistic.
